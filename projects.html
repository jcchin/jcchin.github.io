Project Portfolio

The NASA X-57 Maxwell

Role: GRC Systems Lead

Led the Glenn Research Center's systems analysis for NASA's first all-electric experimental aircraft in two decades. My responsibilities included performance and thermal design for the battery, motor, and power systems. I also led the design and fabrication of several high-performance electric motors and their innovative cooling systems. This project was a critical step in proving the viability of distributed electric propulsion.

Adaptive Cooling for Electric Propulsion

Role: Principal Investigator (PI)

Conceived and led a 5-person team on an Independent R&D award to develop a passive, shape-memory alloy adaptive cooling system. This novel technology was designed to offer a high-reliability, low-weight solution to one of the key challenges in electric propulsion: thermal management.

Autonomously Navigated Experimental Lander

Role: Co-PI & Avionics Lead

As part of an 11-person team, I developed and flew a high-altitude balloon project to test autonomous navigation and landing technologies. This system was designed to significantly improve the recovery rates of multi-million dollar scientific payloads and has since spawned a commercial variant for military applications.

NASA Glenn's Innovation Lab & Makerspace

Role: Founder & Manager

Identified the need for a collaborative, hands-on space for rapid prototyping and founded the Innovation Lab and Makerspace at NASA Glenn. I grew the community to over 100 members, equipping them with the tools and environment to bring their innovative ideas to life.
