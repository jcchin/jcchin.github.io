About Me

For over a decade at the NASA Glenn Research Center, I have been at the forefront of aerospace innovation, leading pioneering research in electric propulsion, advanced battery systems, and transformative aeronautic concepts. My work is driven by a passion for solving complex engineering challenges and a commitment to shaping the future of flight.

I thrive on leading interdisciplinary teams to develop next-generation technology from concept to reality, bridging the gap between foundational research and flight-capable hardware. From leading systems analysis for the all-electric X-57 Maxwell aircraft to founding NASA Glenn's Innovation Lab, my career has been dedicated to pushing the boundaries of what's possible in aviation and beyond.

Key Projects

The NASA X-57 Maxwell

Led systems analysis for NASA's first all-electric aircraft in decades, focusing on performance and thermal design for the battery, motor, and power systems. Led the design and fabrication of several high-performance electric motors and their innovative cooling systems.

Adaptive Cooling for Electric Propulsion

Conceived and led a 5-person team on an Independent R&D award to develop a passive, shape-memory alloy adaptive cooling system, offering a high-reliability, low-weight thermal management solution.

Autonomously Navigated Experimental Lander

Co-led an 11-person team to develop and fly a high-altitude balloon project to test autonomous navigation and landing technologies, which has since spawned a commercial variant for military applications.
